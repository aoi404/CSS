Rizal Wind Farm

Pililla Wind Farm, located in Barangay Halayhayin, features 27 towering turbines spread across rolling hills that overlook Laguna de Bay. Built to harness renewable energy, the site produces 54 megawatts of power—enough to supply thousands of homes. Beyond its function, it has become a popular destination for visitors seeking panoramic views, fresh air, and a closer look at sustainable technology just a short drive from Metro Manila.

Laguna de Bay

Laguna de Bay is the largest lake in the Philippines, covering over 900 square kilometers across the provinces of Rizal, Laguna, and parts of Metro Manila. Shaped like a crow’s foot, the lake is bordered by mountain ranges and volcanic plateaus, with Talim Island at its center. It serves as a vital source of freshwater fish, irrigation, and livelihood for surrounding communities. In Pililla, the lake provides a scenic backdrop to the wind farm and upland areas, offering wide, open views from the hills of Barangay Halayhayin

Diocesan Shrine and Parish of Saint Mary Magdalene

Founded in 1583 by Franciscan missionaries, the Diocesan Shrine and Parish of St. Mary Magdalene is one of the oldest churches in Rizal. Located in Barangay Imatong, it features a simple Baroque façade, a four-story bell tower, and a painted ceiling unique among churches in the province. Declared a diocesan shrine in 2018, it continues to serve as a place of worship and a symbol of Pililla’s enduring spiritual heritage.

Bahay na Bato

This ancestral house, built in 1573 during the Spanish colonial period, stands near the town center in Barangay Imatong. Constructed from stone and hardwood, it reflects the architectural style of the bahay na bato, with capiz windows and a raised wooden upper floor. As one of Pililla’s oldest surviving structures, it offers a glimpse into the town’s early history and remains a quiet landmark beside the St. Mary Magdalene Church.
